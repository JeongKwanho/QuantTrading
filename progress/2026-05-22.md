Designed and implemented the **OBChannelV1** (Bullish Order Block) strategy from scratch, including pattern detection, backtest runner, and chart visualization.

## 1. Pattern Detection — `patterns/order_block.py`

### Detection Logic
- **OB Candle**: A bearish candle where the immediately following bullish candle's body is larger than the bearish candle's body (engulfment condition).
- **Downtrend Filter**: Both LH (Lower High) AND LL (Lower Low) must be satisfied simultaneously within the last `trend_window=30` candles using pivot analysis (`pivot_k=2`).
- **OB Zone**: `ob_close` (body bottom) to `ob_open` (body top) of the bearish OB candle. Wicks are tracked separately (`ob_high`, `ob_low`).

### Invalidation
- **Wick-touch invalidation**: If any candle's `low < ob_low` (below the OB candle's full wick), the OB is immediately marked `valid=False` and cleared on the next evaluation.
- **Blacklist** (`_invalidated_ob_ts`): Once an OB candle is invalidated, its timestamp is stored to prevent re-detection from the same candle.
- **Re-entry after invalidation**: New OB is only accepted if its timestamp differs from the blacklisted one. The blacklist is cleared when a genuinely new OB is confirmed.

### Key Design Decisions
- Used `trend_window=30` (separate from `window=10`) for downtrend detection to avoid false positives from brief dips in uptrends.
- Switched from "3 consecutive LH" to "LH + LL simultaneously" — the 3-consecutive condition paradoxically increased detections because it latched on and stayed true longer.
- Invalidation fires on wick touch (`data.low < ob_low`), not candle close — stricter than standard practice.

## 2. Strategy — `strategies/ob_channel_v1.py`

### Entry (3-Tier Fractional)
| Stage | Trigger | Fraction | Entry Price |
|-------|---------|----------|-------------|
| 1st   | `low <= ob_open` | 25% of balance | `ob_open` |
| 2nd   | `low <= ob_mid`  | 25% of balance | `ob_mid = (ob_open + ob_close) / 2` |
| 3rd   | `low <= ob_close`| 50% of balance | `ob_close` |

- 2nd entry only triggers after 1st is filled; 3rd only after 2nd.
- On the same candle, multiple entries can trigger simultaneously.

### RR Filter
- Calculated at 1st entry: `(tp1_price - ob_open) / (ob_open - ob_low) >= 2.0`
- If RR < 2.0, the entire OB is skipped (all 3 entries blocked) via `_skipped_ob_ts`.

### TP1
- `tp1_price` = max high from the OB candle's next candle up to (and including) the 1st entry candle.
- On TP1 hit: sell 50% of position; SL moves to `avg_price` (SL2 activates).

### TP2
- `tp2_price` = max high of the 7 candles immediately before the OB candle.
- On TP2 hit: sell remaining 100% of position.

### SL / SL2
- **SL** (before TP1): `low <= ob_low` → sell 100% at `ob_low`.
- **SL2** (after TP1): `low <= avg_price` → sell 100% at `avg_price`.

### Exit Priority
- Before TP1: SL checked first, then TP1.
- After TP1: TP2 checked first, then SL2.

## 3. Backtest Runner — `run_backtest_ob.py`

- Fetches up to 10,000 candles from Binance Futures per timeframe.
- Tested scenarios: 1d / 4h / 1h / 15m / 5m.
- **Override price mechanism**: All entries and exits override `broker._current_prices[sym]` to the exact trigger level before placing a market order, then restore to candle close. This ensures fills happen at `ob_open`, `ob_mid`, `ob_close`, `ob_low`, `tp1_price`, `tp2_price`, or `avg_price` — not at candle close.
- Fraction-based sizing: `qty = (broker._balance * fraction) / candle["close"]` at signal time.

## 5. Known Issue (Under Investigation)

**All timeframes returning ~-100%.**

Suspected root cause: `tp2_price` (max of 7 candles *before* OB in a downtrend) is often *lower* than `tp1_price` (max high since OB candle). After TP1 fires at `tp1_price`, the condition `data.high >= tp2_price` is immediately satisfied on the same or next candle because price is already above `tp1_price > tp2_price`. The TP2 override then fills at the lower `tp2_price`, potentially below `avg_price`, causing a loss on every trade.

**Next step**: Verify whether `tp2_price > tp1_price` should be enforced as a pre-condition before entering, or whether the TP2 logic needs to be rethought (e.g., use the swing high *after* the OB as TP2 instead of before).
