## 1. Fixed MockBroker Scaled Entry Accounting

### Problem

`MockBroker` did not correctly handle multiple same-direction entries.

When OBChannelV1 entered in 1st/2nd/3rd legs, each BUY deducted margin and fee from balance, but `_positions[symbol]` was overwritten by the latest entry instead of being accumulated.

This caused earlier entry margin to disappear from later equity calculations, so equity could drop even when the first visible exit was TP1.

### Fix

Updated `broker/mock.py`:

- Same-direction entries now accumulate into one position.
- Entry price is recalculated as weighted average price.
- Position size and margin are accumulated.
- Opposite-side orders now close only the requested quantity.
- Partial close releases proportional margin and realizes proportional PnL.
- Full close removes the position.

### Sanity Check

Manual scenario:

| Step | Result |
|------|--------|
| Buy 10 at 100 | equity = 10000 |
| Buy 10 at 90 | average entry = 95, equity = 9900 |
| Sell 10 at 110 | equity = 10300 |
| Sell remaining 10 at 110 | balance = 10300 |

This confirmed scaled entries and partial exits now behave correctly.

## 3. Added Realistic Futures Cost Model

### Slippage

Set slippage to:

```python
SLIPPAGE = 0.0005  # 0.05%
```

This is applied by `MockBroker` when market orders are filled:

- BUY fills slightly above the target price
- SELL fills slightly below the target price

### Funding Fees

Added funding-fee accounting to `MockBroker`:

- Positive funding rate: longs pay shorts
- Negative funding rate: longs receive from shorts
- Funding is applied only while a position exists
- Funding changes balance directly and is included in final equity/return

`tools/backtest_chart_ob.py` now fetches Binance funding-rate history for each scenario and applies it during the candle loop.

The displayed return is now:

```text
trade PnL
- trading fees
- slippage impact
- paid funding
+ received funding
```

## 5. Current Interpretation

- The previous near `-100%` results were largely caused by incorrect scaled-entry accounting.
- After the broker fix, the strategy becomes profitable before realistic cost modeling.
- With 0.05% slippage, short timeframes degrade sharply.
- 5m and 15m are now negative after realistic costs.
- 1h, 4h, and 1d remain positive in the current sample.
- Funding matters most on longer holds, but slippage is currently the bigger drag on lower timeframes.
